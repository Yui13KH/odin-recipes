Chicken Ramen

Recipe Description

Chicken Ramen Bowl: a rich broth with tender chicken, noodles, and a seven-minute egg for a simple, flavorful meal.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 medium onion, thinly sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon freshly grated ginger
  • ½ cup rice vinegar, or to taste
  • ½ cup reduced-sodium soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons oyster sauce
  • 2 tablespoons mirin
  • 1 tablespoon fish sauce
  • 1 tablespoon Sriracha sauce
  • 4 cups low-sodium chicken broth
  • 1 cup water
  • ¾ pound baby bok choy, quartered lengthwise
  • 1 cooked chicken breast, shredded
  • 3 (3 ounce) packages dried ramen noodles
  • 4 eggs
  • 1 jalapeño pepper, sliced, or to taste
  • 2 green onions, chopped, or to taste
  • ¼ cup chopped cilantro, or to taste

Directions

  1. Heat 1 tablespoon oil in a Dutch oven over medium-high heat. Add onion and sauté until starting to soften, about 5 minutes.
  2. Add garlic and ginger; cook about 30 seconds more.
  3. Add rice vinegar, soy sauce, oyster sauce, mirin, fish sauce, and Sriracha sauce; stir to combine.
  4. Add chicken broth and water and bring to a boil.
  5. Reduce the heat and simmer for about 5 minutes.
  6. Stir in bok choy and chicken; simmer gently until bok choy is just tender, about 2 minutes.
  7. Cover and keep warm.
  8. Fill a large pot with lightly salted water and bring to a rolling boil.
  9. Add ramen noodles and simmer until tender, about 3 minutes.
